Mission

Our mission is to develop assistive technologies that can help elderly that need aid with certain tasks, or patients who may have special needs that can be taken care of with assistive technology.

We intend to build systems that provide both physical assistance and emotional support, performing tasks such as tracking vital signs, monitoring health status, managing medications, supporting mobility, and offering companionship.

By prioritizing safe, supportive, and practical assistive functions, we aim to advance the future of compassionate and accessible healthcare technology.

Vision

Our long-term vision is to develop a functional humanoid caregiver robot designed to assist with everyday tasks that support comfort, independence, and overall well-being.

The robot will focus on reliable, human-centered capabilities such as mobility support, routine assistance, health monitoring, and meaningful user interaction.

About Us

BananaBots is a student-led organization founded by students at UC Santa Cruz dedicated to advancing the future of assistive robotics in healthcare technology.

By combining hands-on engineering with real-world problem-solving, we aim to create technologies that enhance independence, safety, and quality of life for elderly individuals and patients recovering from medical procedures.

BananaBots offers opportunities for students of all experience levels to learn, create, and contribute to meaningful projects with real social value.

BananaPuck

What is BananaPuck?

BananaPuck is a chest-worn adhesive monitoring device designed for elderly individuals and patients recovering from injury or surgery. It continuously tracks key physiological signals and movement to support early detection of health risks.

Why It Matters

The device captures early warning signs of medical incidents, allowing caregivers to intervene before emergencies occur. BananaPuck promotes safety while supporting independence.

How It Works

Embedded sensors collect data on heart rate, respiration, temperature, movement, orientation, and location. Sensor data is processed by an onboard microcontroller and wirelessly transmitted to a secure cloud server. Caregivers access the data through a dashboard that displays real-time status, alerts for abnormal readings, and historical trends for longer-term monitoring.
